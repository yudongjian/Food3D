Food3D: Generating 3D Food Images Using a Dual-Branch Diffusion Model

Abstract

This paper proposes a 3D food image generation method based on a dual-branch diffusion model, referred to as Food3D. The primary challenge in generating 3D food images lies in the significant disparity between food images generated by existing 2D text-to-image models and real food images. To address this issue, we utilized the DreamBooth model to generate realistic 2D food images; however, this approach only yields single-view perspectives. Simultaneously, while text-to-3D models can accurately capture the approximate shape of the food, they fall short in texture and appearance. To overcome these limitations, we propose a dual-branch diffusion model that generates shape information (Shape-SD) and texture information (Texture-SD) separately. Additionally, to further enhance the generation effect, we introduce a classifier-free guidance scheduling algorithm to better coordinate the generation of shape and texture. Experimental results demonstrate that this method achieves significant improvements in shape, detail, and overall generation quality.
