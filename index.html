Food3D: Text-Driven Customizable 3D Food Generation
with Gaussian Splatting


Dongjian Yu ① Wenqing Min ② Xin Jin ①

Qiang Jiang ① Shaowen Yao ① Shuqiang Jiang ②

① Yunnan University

② Key Laboratory of Intelligent Information Processing, Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences

Abstract

Realistic 3D food creation is essential for nutritional assessment, advertising, and other applications. The existing text-to-3D models initialize a 3D representation and use a text-to- image model as supervision to render it, ultimately obtaining the final 3D representation. However, these models has two main limitations. This paper introduces Food3D, a new framework for 3D food generation, addressing two main limitations. 1) Limitations of Initialization in 3D Generation: Poor initialization can result in the generated 3D food lacking key details and realism, thereby reducing their quality. To solve this, we propose Food3D-G, a method based on Mamba initialization. This method improves the initialization process and enhances the overall quality of generated 3D food models. 2) Limitations of Text- to-Image Models: Current text-to-3D models often use text-to- image models for supervision. However, there is a significant gap between the images generated by these models and real images, particularly for complex foods. They fail to capture the fine details and textures, which affects the quality of the 3D food. To address this, we propose Food3D-C, a customizable solution for generating personalized 3D food models. Food3D- C uses a dual-branch diffusion model to capture more details, improving results for complex foods. Both methods use 3D Gaussian splatting (3D GS) and a Schedulable Interval Score Matching (S-ISM) algorithm to improve shape and texture generation. Our experiments show that Food3D achieves state- of-the-art performance, with significant improvements in detail, shape accuracy, and realism.
